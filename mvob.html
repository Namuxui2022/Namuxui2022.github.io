Introduction
After many years of working with families experiencing homelessness, Michael Allen founded My Very Own Bed in 2014 with one goal in mind: to provide new beds to children in families who have recently moved into stable housing.
My role
I (Nam Phansri) UX/UI design and led UI design, collaborated with 2 teammates (Jeremiah Dean and Zola Pinneles) during the early ideation phase,  conducted user research, user testing, and developed a website for reconstructing and analysis. The TA, Camilla S., also coached us on this project.
Problem
My Very Own Bed is a non-profit dedicated to giving children a bed as they transition out of homelessness—and its website is meant to promote that mission and encourage visitors to donate, volunteer, or engage with the organization. Our observations tell us a prominent mission statement; linked social media accounts; and clearer, more persistent CTAs may convert users from visiting to giving.
How might we improve My Very Own Bed’s user journey so customers engage with the site, as measured by an increase in donations, volunteer sign ups, and social media followers?
The previous landing page did not establish enough trust with users.
According to our study, users want to learn more about an organization before contributing, such as its purpose or effect. Nevertheless, the outdated landing page did not demonstrate the organization's goals, which caused visitors to abandon the page.
Volunteer and donate CTA buttons are confused by their varied directions.
To comprehend the founder would want to encourage users to volunteer and donate since the site is poorly organized and the interior layout appears too overwhelming by employing too many buttons to volunteer and donate, which is too much for users to process and go forward with.

Introduction
In this project, Imagine that I am just been hired as a product designer for a new travel startup. The startup’s goal is to build a modern-day mobile app that helps people plan their next trip, post-pandemic.
My role
I (Nam Phansri) UX/UI designer, during the early ideation phase,  conducted user research, user testing, and developed mobile app. The TA, Rick Miller., also coached me on this project.
Problem
I've noticed that neither travel websites nor applications are user-friendly nor do they cater to the demands of travelers. When a traveler already concluded the offers were gone because they were time-sensitive, they would not finish their booking and become  overburdened.
How might we create travel mobile app so that allows all users to discover new places and create the ultimate travel experience. With smooth easy to use and customizable interface and notifications for everyone on one platform?
Solution and Outcome
Created simple access for users to discover
After conducting user research, user overwhelmed and not complete their booking after they have to search on different site by the time they already decided the deals were gone because they are time sensitive. Pegasus will Includes well-organized CTA buttons that make it simple for visitors to obtain the information they need for their trip, as well as an eye-catching travel banner with bargains on the site to help consumers decide where they want to go.
Promote vacation packages
According to user research, the majority of users are searching for affordable travel options. In order to accomplish their aim and save time and money, they would love to see more package offers.  Pegasus creates CTA buttons for package offers that contain items like hotel + flight, hotel + flight + rental vehicle, etc. In this manner, the consumer will avoid having to hunt for offers on other travel websites.

MY ROLE

Introduction
To acquire a thought for a brand-new mobile application, use this project. A work must meet an actual, definite need that users have in their daily lives. The application that helps dog owners locate pet-friendly locations received the majority of the vote in my group. DogGo will develop to promote pet-friendly venues and inspire dog owners to play with their pets more frequently.
My role
I (Nam Phansri) UX/UI design, collaborated with 3 teammates (Chelsea Sullivan, Laura Steinkopf, Tia-Tamera Lester) during the early ideation phase,  conducted user research, user testing, and developed mobile app. The TA, Olcay Paull., also coached us on this project.
Problem
It is challenging for dog owners to find accurate information about dog parks and other dog-friendly places. Whether planning a dog park visit or heading to the local brewery patio, there can be uncertainty about the rules, amenities, safety, and crowds.
How might we develop a trustworthy application that increases the credibility and confidence of dog owners while making it simple to locate dog parks, activities, and companies that welcome canines?
Solution and Outcome
Created simple access to locate dog parks, activities, and companies that welcome canine.
Our study suggests that people can be unaware of wonderful pet-friendly places in their local area, and that misleading website information has frustrated users. DogGo designed simple access buttons that organized locations and activities that dog owners and their canines could partake in. Additionally, current details at each location, such as hours, addresses, popular times to visit, and dog amenities  etc.
Reliable and boosts dog owners' trustworthiness and confidence
According to our survey, a large majority of users like to read reviews before making a visit decision and frequently recommend businesses to others. DogGo created a top review to encourage users to interact with the app, and allow users to add new information and review.
